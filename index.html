Design Showcase

Selected UI/UX projects with a direct Figma view for your review.

HRIS (Human Resource Information System)

This comprehensive HRIS is an end-to-end platform designed to centralize and streamline core employee data management. The primary UX challenge was transforming complex administrative tasks into an intuitive experience for both HR professionals and general staff. By structuring seamless modules for automated payroll processing, real-time attendance tracking, and efficient leave management, the design significantly reduces administrative friction and delivers a clean, user-friendly workspace.

See in Figma

OSIMAP (Optimized Spatial Information Map for Accident Prevention)

OSIMAP is a cross-platform (Web & Android) application designed to enhance road safety in San Fernando, Pampanga. The core UX challenge was translating dense, complex spatial data into an intuitive, low-cognitive-load interface for drivers. By utilizing high-contrast visual hierarchies, dynamic heatmap visualizations, and real-time contextual alerts, the design empowers users with glanceable, immediate awareness of high-risk accident hotspots without causing dangerous distractions.

See in Figma

MELLO (Mindfulness, Empathy, Listening, and Letting Out)

Mello is a mental health application designed to provide accessible, multi-tiered emotional support. The core UX challenge was creating a calming, low-friction environment for users who may be experiencing active distress. By utilizing empathetic microcopy, intuitive navigation hierarchies, and seamless transitions between licensed therapy, anonymous communities, and on-demand listeners, the design empowers users to find immediate comfort and professional guidance without cognitive overload.

How I Explain My Design

A short breakdown of my end-to-end UX process.

Empathize

First, I study user needs, pain points, and behavior through interviews, surveys, and competitor analysis.

Define

Second, I synthesize research into clear problem statements, personas, and measurable success criteria.

Ideate

Third, I explore references, brainstorm options, and sketch concepts before committing to polished UI.

Prototype

Fourth, I build wireframes, apply visual design decisions, and create interactive prototypes for review.

Test

Fifth, I validate with users or stakeholders, gather feedback, and iterate until the experience works better.
